Methodology & Theory

The mechanics of hunting institutional momentum.

The Core Philosophy

This scanner is a motion detector, not a portfolio manager. It is designed to find institutional accumulation in mid-cap and small-cap stocks. Because institutions move millions of dollars, they cannot hide their trades—they leave a massive footprint in the daily volume. We are mathematically hunting for that exact footprint occurring within an established uptrend, signaling a high-probability breakout.

The Mathematical Funnel

1. The Universe

S&P 400 (Mid-Cap) and S&P 600 (Small-Cap). We ignore mega-caps (too slow) and unlisted micro-caps (pump-and-dump risk).

2. Baseline Viability

Price > $10 and > 50 days of trading history. Filters out penny stocks and erratic newly IPO'd companies.

3. Institutional Liquidity

10-Day Average Volume > 500,000 shares, AND Today's Volume > 1,000,000 shares. Ensures you can exit trades easily and prevents the scanner from being tricked by "dead" barcode charts.

4. The Trend & The Floor

20-day EMA > 50-day EMA (short-term momentum is accelerating). Current Price > 20-day EMA (the stock is holding its immediate support floor).

5. The Trigger (Accumulation)

Today's Volume > 1.5x Average AND the stock closed green today. This is the spark. It confirms buyers took aggressive control of the price.

Execution Playbook

  • The Pivot: Find the previous ceiling the stock just broke through. That ceiling is your new floor.
  • Avoid the Open: Do not buy at 9:30 AM. The morning open is erratic and designed to hunt retail stop-losses.
  • Power Hour Execution: Check the chart at 3:45 PM the following day. If the stock is holding above the Pivot and showing strength, execute the buy.
  • The Macro Filter (VIX): If the VIX is > 22, the broader market is hostile. Breakouts will fail. The scanner will warn you to hold cash.
